# MADabolic Franchisee Data Collection

A structured set of questions for gathering location-level data from other MADabolic franchisees. The goal is to build out a benchmark dataset so the site selection model can pattern-match new prospective locations against real performance data across the system, not just demographics on paper.

The questions are organized in tiers. Tier 1 is the must-have minimum. Tier 2 adds context. Tier 3 is nice-to-have if the operator is willing.

---

## Tier 1: Core Performance Data

These are the questions that matter most for benchmarking. If a franchisee only answers this section, the data is still useful.

### Location Basics
1. Studio name and full street address
2. Year and month opened
3. Square footage
4. Class capacity (max members per class)
5. Number of classes offered per week
6. Number of coaches on staff
7. Is this a single-unit or multi-unit operation? If multi-unit, how many?

### Membership Performance
8. Current active member count
9. Peak member count ever achieved
10. Average member count over the last 12 months
11. Approximate breakdown of membership tiers (month-to-month vs 6-month vs 12-month)
12. Average length of stay for active members in months
13. Annual churn rate as a percentage

### Conversion Funnel
14. Average leads per month from all sources
15. Approximate trial-to-membership conversion rate
16. Most common first-touch lead source (Google, Instagram, Meta ads, referral, walk-in, other)
17. Approximate cost per acquisition

### Pricing
18. Current month-to-month price
19. Current 6-month and 12-month commitment prices
20. Are you running any active promotions or discounts? If so, what?

---

## Tier 2: Market and Operational Context

These help explain why a location performs the way it does.

### Member Profile
21. Approximate male / female split
22. Estimated average age of active members
23. Top three professional categories represented (finance, tech, government, healthcare, hospitality, etc.)
24. What percentage of members live within a 1-mile radius? Within a 3-mile radius?
25. What percentage of members come from work versus home?

### Competition
26. List of major boutique fitness competitors within 1 mile (F45, Barry's, OTF, Solidcore, Burn Boot Camp, CrossFit, Equinox, etc.)
27. List of major boutique fitness competitors within 3 miles
28. Have any direct competitors opened or closed within the last 24 months?
29. How would you rate local competition saturation on a 1-5 scale (1 = no competition, 5 = oversaturated)?

### Site Characteristics
30. Type of property (street-level retail, second-floor walk-up, mall, mixed-use, standalone)
31. Parking situation (street, dedicated lot, paid garage, walk-only)
32. Is the studio visible from the street?
33. What are the closest daily anchors within 2 blocks (coffee shop, grocery store, residential, office, etc.)?
34. Walk Score for the address
35. What is the primary commute pattern of your members (drive, walk, transit, bike)?

### Neighborhood Type
36. How would you describe the neighborhood archetype?
   - Affluent urban village (walkable, stable, professional)
   - Dense urban core (high traffic, more transient)
   - Trendy/nightlife district
   - High-end suburban node
   - Mixed-use development
   - Other (please describe)
37. Is the surrounding area mostly homeowners or renters?
38. Has the neighborhood gotten more or less stable in the last 3 years?

---

## Tier 3: Deep Operator Insights

These are open-ended and most useful if the franchisee will jump on a 30-minute call.

### Performance Reflection
39. Are you outperforming, underperforming, or roughly hitting the proforma you opened with?
40. What is the single biggest driver of retention at your location?
41. What is the single biggest driver of churn?
42. If you could go back, would you pick the same address? Why or why not?

### Coaching and Operations
43. How long has your current GM been in role?
44. How long is your average coach tenure?
45. Have you experienced significant coaching turnover? If yes, when and what was the impact?
46. How would you describe your coaching culture in one sentence?

### Brand and Messaging
47. What language do you use locally to explain MADabolic to first-time prospects?
48. Do you find the official MADabolic terminology (Momentum, Anaerobic, Durability) helps or hurts conversion?
49. What objection do you hear most often from prospects?

### Lessons Learned
50. What is one thing you wish you knew about your market before opening?
51. What demographic or behavioral pattern did you not expect that has shaped your business?
52. What would you tell a franchisee considering a similar market?

## Suggested Outreach Approach

Two ways to use this:

**Survey path.** Send Tier 1 and Tier 2 as a Google Form or Typeform. Aim for 10 to 15 responses across the system. This gives you a real benchmark dataset.

**Interview path.** Pick 3 to 5 high-performing operators and 2 to 3 underperformers. Run 30-minute Zooms covering Tier 1, 2, and the open-ended Tier 3 questions. Record with permission so you can mine the audio later.

The survey gives you breadth. The interviews give you depth. Both feed the model.

---

## How This Data Plugs Into the Site Selection Tool

Each new location entered into the tool gets scored against a weighted framework. Franchisee data is what calibrates the weights. For example, if the data shows that homeownership percentage correlates 0.7+ with member length-of-stay across 15 locations, the model can confidently weight stability that heavily. Without the data, weighting is informed guessing.

The goal is to move from intuition to evidence over the next 6 to 12 months.
